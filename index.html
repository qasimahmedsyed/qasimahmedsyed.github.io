Research

At the seam of perception, memory & autonomy

Toward multimodal deep learning that perceives and reasons.

My research is converging on multimodal machine learning, models that jointly perceive and reason across vision and language rather than treating each modality in isolation. I am drawn to how learned representations can bind what a model sees to what it reads and retrieves, and to the questions that follow: how do we make such models robust, efficient, and trustworthy under the noisy, unconstrained conditions of the real world?

This direction grows naturally out of my work on both sides of the modality gap. On perception, I studied deep-learning face detection in the wild and knowledge-based image enhancement and denoising; on language, I worked on text representation and on retrieval-augmented, agentic systems that ground generation in verifiable sources. Multimodal learning is where these threads meet.

For PhD, I want to pursue multimodal representation learning including but not limited to vision-language models, grounding language in perception, retrieval-augmented multimodal reasoning, and methods that stay reliable in low-resource and out-of-distribution settings.
